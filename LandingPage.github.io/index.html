Traditional silkwkorm farmers

Traditional silkworm farmers are very protective of their animals and meticolously shelter them from the outside world. It is for instance considered very bad if the silkworms are exposed to shouting.
Traditionally, they are kept in a warm and dry place for eight or nine days before being boiled.

Stages of production

The female silk moth lays circa 300-500 eggs on a mullberry tree. Larvae (caterpillars) hatch out of the eggs. They are called silkworms.
After having gone through its fourth moult, the silkworm is ready to transform into a moth. It climbs to a twig and starts extruding a silk fiber to spin a net.

The cocoons

Silk is a natural protein fiber composed mainly of fibroin. It is produced by certain insect larvae who use it to create their cocoons. The best-known silk is the type obtained from the larvae of the species.
In English, this insect is known as Domestic silkmoth, and the larvae are referred to as silkworms even though they aren’t worms.

The silk solidifies when it comes into contact with the air. The silkworm gradually encloses itself in a cocoon. Making a complete cocoon.
